This document is the highest project authority for TimeProofs. It governs the complete global company and product system, not only the open protocol.

1. an open evidence protocol;
2. a reference verifier and developer platform;
3. a managed outcome-assurance service;
4. an interoperability layer for agentic actions and commerce;

## 2. Mission

> TimeProofs verifies, tracks, and preserves evidence of the real outcomes of actions executed by AI agents.

Canonical promise:

> When an AI says “done”, verify what actually happened.

## 3. Category

Working category:

> Outcome Assurance for AI Actions

The category covers the evidence required to distinguish between:

- an agent intending to act;
- an action being authorized;
- a request being dispatched;
- a receiver accepting it;
- a business effect being recorded;
- a final outcome being settled;
- an outcome later being cancelled, reversed, contradicted, or becoming unprovable.

## 4. Core architecture

```text
Immutable evidence
        ↓
Structural and cryptographic verification
        ↓
Versioned domain rules
        ↓
Recalculable outcome verdict
        ↓
Human Evidence Packet and machine API
        ↓
Long-term retention and re-verification
```

The layers MUST remain separable. A cryptographically valid bundle may still be insufficient or contradictory for a business verdict.

## 7. Product boundaries

TimeProofs is not, unless a future approved strategy explicitly changes the boundary:

- a generic observability platform;
- a payment processor or wallet;
- an identity provider;
- a universal authorization firewall;
- a model evaluation product;
- a proprietary blockchain;
- an insurance carrier;
- a legal truth oracle;
- a claim that cryptographic integrity alone establishes factual truth.

## 8. Initial wedge and expansion

The first domain is refund lifecycle assurance because it makes the accountability gap visible:

```text
refund requested
≠ refund object created
≠ settlement submitted
≠ funds settled
≠ result still valid after reversals
```

The wedge is not the final market. Expansion proceeds to:

- email and messaging;
- appointments and reservations;
- purchases and fulfillment;
- account and subscription changes;
- claims, approvals, support, and enterprise workflows;
- multi-agent delegation and other consequential actions.

## 9. Agentic commerce position

TimeProofs MUST complement rather than replace commerce and action protocols.

It must be able to map evidence from systems such as ACP, UCP, AP2, MCP, A2A, HTTP APIs, webhooks, payment service providers, and merchant systems of record into a portable outcome dossier.

The durable value is cross-boundary evidence and outcome assurance, not ownership of checkout or payment authorization.

## 10. Moat strategy

The open syntax is not the moat. Defensibility must compound through:

1. integrations with systems of record;
2. issuer, key, and evidence-source registry;
3. domain rules and contradiction intelligence;
4. lifecycle correlation across protocols;
5. long-term retention and re-verification;
6. interoperability credibility and independent implementations;
7. developer adoption and category vocabulary;
8. a trusted badge whose meaning is precise and inspectable;
9. global documentation and AI-first discoverability;
10. accumulated evidence-quality corpus and compatibility tests.

## 11. Business objective

The product must develop a credible path to at least EUR 10,000 monthly recurring revenue while remaining compatible with a lean, highly automated operation.

The launch model combines subscription and usage. Pure per-proof microbilling is a scale-stage mechanism, not the initial dependency.

No price becomes public until costs, entitlements, support boundaries, legal terms, and purchase flows pass the commercial gate.

## 12. Execution principles

- One unique next action at a time.
- Protocol correctness before dashboard breadth.
- A real system-of-record signal before claims of verified outcome.
- Tests and evidence before marketing claims.
- Open standards before proprietary cryptography.
- Product-led distribution before dependence on outbound sales.
- Small, reversible milestones before infrastructure expansion.
- Competition and standards reviewed continuously.
- Website and content designed for humans, search engines, and AI systems.

## 13. Global success conditions

TimeProofs is not considered a credible global product until it has:

- two independent interoperable implementations;
- a clear, non-technical demonstration of outcome ambiguity;
- a five-minute developer integration path;
- at least one real sandbox integration with a system of record;
- secure key lifecycle and evidence retention design;
- a public verifier and evidence packet;
- documented compatibility with major agentic action protocols;
- external users or design partners producing real bundles;
- validated willingness to pay;
- a global site with trustworthy technical and legal surfaces;
- repeatable acquisition channels and measurable product usage.
