# Release Gates

No release label may imply more maturity than the gates actually passed.

## Gate G0 — Global operating system

Requirements:

- global authority;
- machine-readable state;
- unique next action;
- risk, decision, assumption, competition, integration, site, business, and operations programs;
- CI validation.

Current status: PASS.

## Gate G1 — Interoperable protocol alpha

Requirements:

- complete normative Evidence Bundle semantics;
- aligned schemas and stable error codes;
- canonicalization and signature test vectors;
- second independent implementation;
- independent security review or documented review plan with no unresolved critical issue.

Public claim allowed after pass: experimental interoperable protocol alpha.

## Gate G2 — Outcome demonstration

Requirements:

- machine bundles and human packets share one source;
- pending, verified, contradicted, reversed, and unprovable states;
- source and missing-evidence visibility;
- non-technical comprehension test.

Public claim allowed after pass: working outcome-assurance demonstration.

## Gate G3 — Developer alpha

Requirements:

- Node and Python SDKs;
- HTTP and MCP integration examples;
- under-ten-minute independent integration test;
- secure defaults and clear failure behavior;
- package supply-chain controls.

Public claim allowed after pass: developer alpha.

## Gate G4 — Managed service alpha

Requirements:

- approved threat and privacy model;
- key rotation and revocation;
- retention and deletion policies;
- backup and restore test;
- access control and audit logs;
- incident procedure;
- cost instrumentation.

Public claim allowed after pass: managed private alpha.

## Gate G5 — Market validation

Requirements:

- external real or sandbox workflows;
- at least one system-of-record connector;
- repeated external use;
- documented willingness to pay or paid user;
- support burden understood.

Public claim allowed after pass: validated early product.

## Gate G6 — Global public launch

Requirements:

- reliable self-service product;
- legal, privacy, security, support, pricing, and status surfaces;
- global site QA, SEO, GEO, accessibility, and internationalization foundations;
- no unsupported compliance, customer, benchmark, or standards claims;
- launch rollback plan.

Public claim allowed after pass: public product, with maturity qualifier matching service state.
