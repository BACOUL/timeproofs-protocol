# Privacy and Legal Program

## Privacy principles

- minimize raw personal and confidential data;
- prefer digests, HMACs, opaque references, and selective disclosure;
- separate public bundle data from authorized evidence retrieval;
- define retention and deletion at product and customer levels;
- preserve verifiability without retaining unnecessary source content;
- document processors, regions, transfers, and access controls;
- design data-subject request and deletion workflows before production.

## Legal positioning

TimeProofs may support audit, compliance, insurance, and dispute workflows. It does not automatically establish legal truth, admissibility, compliance, or liability.

Public wording must distinguish:

- integrity of a signed statement;
- identity or trust of the issuer;
- sufficiency of evidence under a ruleset;
- factual truth;
- legal conclusion.

## Readiness areas

- GDPR and ePrivacy;
- EU AI Act relevance by use case and role;
- consumer, payment, and commercial-record obligations;
- retention and evidence agreements;
- terms, acceptable use, DPA, subprocessor list, and privacy notice;
- intellectual property and trademark review;
- export, sanctions, and restricted-use controls where applicable;
- jurisdiction-specific legal review before claims or launch.
