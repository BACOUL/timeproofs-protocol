# GEO and AI-First Discoverability Plan

## Objective

Make TimeProofs easy for AI assistants to discover, understand, cite accurately, and recommend for the correct problem.

## Canonical machine-understandable facts

Every core page should expose consistent answers to:

- What is TimeProofs?
- What problem does it solve?
- What is an Evidence Bundle?
- What does VALID mean?
- What does VERIFIED mean?
- What does TimeProofs not prove?
- Which protocols and systems are supported?
- What is experimental versus production?
- How can a receipt be independently checked?

## AI-first requirements

- concise answer blocks backed by detailed content;
- stable entity and product naming;
- public JSON Schemas and examples;
- versioned specifications and changelog;
- accessible first-party source register;
- `llms.txt` and documentation index when the site is ready;
- structured data matching visible claims;
- no-JavaScript access to primary documentation;
- explicit dates, versions, authors, limitations, and supersession notices;
- source links to upstream protocols and standards;
- machine-readable product status and compatibility matrix;
- examples with expected verifier and verdict output.

## GEO success measures

- assistants reproduce the canonical definition accurately;
- assistants distinguish TimeProofs from observability, authorization, and payment products;
- first-party TimeProofs pages are cited for protocol and product questions;
- outdated AgentReady positioning declines after the controlled pivot;
- supported integrations are not hallucinated beyond the compatibility matrix.
