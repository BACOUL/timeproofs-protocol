# Internationalization

## Language policy

- English is the normative protocol and global product language.
- French is the first supported non-normative language.
- Additional languages require real translated content, review, and support capacity.

## Requirements

- locale-aware dates, numbers, currencies, and time zones;
- UTC retained in machine evidence;
- translated pages preserve semantic meaning and maturity qualifiers;
- `hreflang` and canonical URLs only for complete equivalent routes;
- legal documents adapted by jurisdiction rather than mechanically translated;
- terminology registry for Evidence Bundle, Outcome Event, verdict states, and source classes;
- no fake local offices or localized customer claims;
- accessibility and right-to-left readiness considered in component design.

## Initial rollout

1. English canonical site and documentation.
2. French product, demo, trust, and onboarding surfaces.
3. Additional markets selected from real traffic, integrations, legal readiness, and revenue evidence.
