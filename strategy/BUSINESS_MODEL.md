# Business Model Strategy

Status: hypotheses, not public pricing.

## Open adoption layer

Free and open:

- protocol specifications;
- schemas and test vectors;
- local verifier;
- base SDKs;
- examples and public verification semantics.

Purpose: reduce adoption friction, enable independent verification, and prevent the protocol from depending on vendor trust.

## Paid assurance layer

Potential paid capabilities:

- managed issuer and key lifecycle;
- trusted timestamps and transparency anchoring;
- evidence retention and retrieval;
- automatic re-verification;
- system-of-record connectors;
- lifecycle correlation and contradiction alerts;
- domain and organization rulesets;
- Outcome Evidence Packets and dispute exports;
- team controls, access policies, audit history, and SLA;
- high-volume evidence operations.

## Launch economics

Pure microbilling is not the launch dependency. Early packaging should combine subscription and included usage.

Revenue paths to test, not publish yet:

- 100 customers at approximately EUR 99 MRR;
- 25 customers at approximately EUR 399 MRR;
- mixed developer, connector, retention, and compliance packages;
- usage expansion once automatic volume exists.

## Required validation

Before publishing pricing:

- buyer and user identified;
- activation event measured;
- cost per bundle, storage period, verification, and connector understood;
- support entitlement bounded;
- gross-margin target modeled;
- willingness to pay observed;
- self-service billing and cancellation designed;
- legal and tax surfaces ready.

## Business anti-patterns

- selling a JSON file;
- requiring manual consulting for the standard product;
- pricing only by fractions of a cent before volume;
- enterprise claims before enterprise operations exist;
- unlimited retention without cost controls;
- custom integrations that turn the company into an agency.
