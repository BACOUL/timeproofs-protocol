# Category and Positioning

## Category

**Outcome Assurance for AI Actions**

## Canonical definition

TimeProofs verifies, tracks, and preserves evidence of the real outcomes of actions executed by AI agents.

## Problem statement

Agents increasingly move from advice to action. Technical traces can show that a model or tool call happened, but they may not establish what the receiving system accepted, what the business system recorded, whether settlement completed, or whether the result was later reversed.

## Position

TimeProofs sits between agent activity and business accountability:

```text
Agent claim and tool trace
          ↓
receiver and system-of-record evidence
          ↓
TimeProofs verification and outcome rules
          ↓
inspectable outcome status
```

## Primary audiences by phase

1. agent and tool developers;
2. operators of consequential workflows;
3. commerce and payment integrators;
4. security, risk, compliance, and audit teams;
5. platforms and protocol implementers.

## Messaging hierarchy

### One sentence

> When an AI says “done”, TimeProofs verifies what actually happened.

### Technical

> An open evidence format, verifier, and managed assurance layer for the lifecycle of consequential AI actions.

### Business

> Replace “the agent says it worked” with a record linked to the systems that recognize the real outcome.

## Prohibited positioning

Do not claim:

- universal truth;
- guaranteed compliance;
- official certification;
- fraud prevention without a validated prevention product;
- legal admissibility in every jurisdiction;
- support for integrations or standards not implemented and tested.
