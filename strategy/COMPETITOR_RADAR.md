## Structural competitors

- native receipts inside ACP, UCP, AP2, MCP tools, A2A tasks, payment processors, commerce platforms, and enterprise systems;
- observability platforms extending into outcome verification;
- governance platforms adding evidence packets;
- standards profiles that commoditize the wire format.

## Monthly questions

1. What became a commodity?
2. Who added receiver or system-of-record evidence?
3. Who added long-term verification or key history?
4. Who gained credible integrations, design partners, funding, or distribution?
5. Which upstream protocol now proves more of the lifecycle?
6. Does the initial refund wedge remain differentiating?
7. Which TimeProofs feature should be removed, accelerated, or converted into compatibility work?

## Competitive rule

Competition confirms demand but does not validate TimeProofs' differentiation or economics. The project must earn its position through working interoperability, stronger outcome semantics, and adoption evidence.
